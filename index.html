Raja Raja Cholan

Raja Raja Cholan, one of the greatest kings of South India, was the ruler of the Chola dynasty from 985 to 1014 CE. His reign marked the peak of Chola power and culture, expanding the Chola empire's reach from the southern tip of India to parts of Sri Lanka and the Maldives. Known for his military conquests, administrative excellence, and patronage of Tamil culture, Raja Raja Cholan's legacy includes the construction of the magnificent Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a marvel of Dravidian architecture.
